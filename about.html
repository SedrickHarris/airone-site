Local Knowledge

Why Southern California HVAC Is Different - And Why It Matters

HVAC in Los Angeles is not the same as HVAC in Phoenix, Dallas, or Chicago. The marine layer in Manhattan Beach creates humidity conditions that accelerate coil corrosion differently than the dry desert heat in Palmdale. AIRONE technicians are trained specifically for Southern California conditions.

What Is Title 24 and How Does It Affect My HVAC Installation?

California's Title 24 energy code governs HVAC installations statewide, setting minimum efficiency standards for all new and replacement equipment. Every AIRONE installation is designed, sized, and permitted in full compliance with Title 24 - protecting homeowners from failed inspections, permit violations, and insurance complications that unlicensed or non-compliant installations create. If a contractor quotes a lower price by skipping Title 24 compliance, the long-term cost to the homeowner is significantly higher.

How Do SCE and SoCalGas Rates Affect Which HVAC System I Should Buy?

AIRONE factors current Southern California Edison tiered and time-of-use electricity rates into every system recommendation. A higher-SEER AC system that costs $2,000 more upfront may save $400-$600 per year on an SCE bill under current tiered rates - a 3-5 year payback that a price-only recommendation misses. For homeowners considering heat pump systems, AIRONE models the transition from SoCalGas to all-electric operation and its impact on monthly utility costs before recommending a system.

Do Coastal Homes in Los Angeles Need Special HVAC Equipment?

Yes. Salt air accelerates corrosion on condenser coils and electrical components in coastal South Bay and Orange County cities. AIRONE recommends and installs coastal-rated HVAC equipment - with coated coils, corrosion-resistant cabinets, and marine-grade electrical components - for homes within 1-2 miles of the ocean. Standard equipment installed in coastal environments without corrosion protection can fail years ahead of its rated lifespan.

How Does AIRONE Protect Homes During Wildfire Season?

Los Angeles County and Orange County face active wildfire smoke events every year. According to the EPA, wildfire smoke particles range from 0.4-0.7 microns - small enough to bypass standard HVAC filters. AIRONE recommends MERV 13 or higher filtration for most SoCal homes during wildfire season, combined with duct sealing to prevent smoke infiltration. For homes with severe sensitivity, whole-home air purifiers and UV sanitizers provide an additional layer of protection.
