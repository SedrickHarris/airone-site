Why Indoor Air Quality Is a Real Problem in Southern California

Southern California has unique IAQ challenges that most homeowners don’t see coming - wildfire smoke season, coastal humidity, and sealed modern homes that trap pollutants inside.

How polluted is the air inside a typical LA home?

According to the EPA, indoor air can be 2 to 5 times more polluted than outdoor air. In Southern California, this is compounded by sealed, energy-efficient homes that limit fresh air exchange - trapping dust, pet dander, VOCs from building materials, and recirculated HVAC debris inside. Homes without filtration upgrades accumulate contaminants every time the HVAC system runs.

Does wildfire smoke actually enter my home through the HVAC system?

Yes. During wildfire events - which the EPA now considers a major source of fine particle pollution in California, capable of reaching PM2.5 concentrations 10 times the healthy threshold indoors - fine smoke particles enter homes through HVAC air intakes, duct leaks, and building envelope gaps. Standard MERV 8 filters do not capture PM2.5 particles. MERV 13 filtration and whole-home air purifiers significantly reduce indoor wildfire smoke concentration. AIRONE assesses your current HVAC system to confirm it can support higher-efficiency filtration before upgrading.

Learn About Air Purifier Options →

How does coastal humidity affect ductwork and air quality?

South Bay and Orange County homes experience elevated coastal humidity year-round. This creates favorable conditions for mold and mildew growth inside duct systems - particularly in older flex duct installations where condensation can accumulate. Contaminated ducts recirculate mold spores through every room of the home. Regular duct cleaning and duct sealing address both the biological contamination and the air leakage that allows moisture into the system.

Product Details

What Each IAQ Service Actually Does

Understanding the difference between filtration, purification, UV sanitization, and duct services helps you choose the right combination for your home.

Air Purifiers - Whole-Home Filtration vs. Room Purifiers

Portable room air purifiers treat a single room. Whole-home air purifiers install directly into your HVAC system and treat every cubic foot of air that passes through your home’s HVAC circulation. Technologies include HEPA media, MERV 13 enhanced media, electronic precipitation, and photocatalytic oxidation (PCO). AIRONE selects the right technology based on your existing HVAC equipment capacity and your household’s specific air quality concerns.

View Air Purifier Installation →

UV-C Germicidal Lights - How They Work and Where They Install

UV-C germicidal lamps emit ultraviolet light at a wavelength that damages the DNA of airborne microorganisms - bacteria, viruses, and mold spores - rendering them unable to reproduce. The lamps install inside the air handler, typically near the evaporator coil or in the supply plenum. Single-lamp configurations target the coil surface; dual-lamp configurations add treatment of air passing through the supply plenum. UV light sanitizers work best alongside MERV 13 filtration - filtration captures particles, UV-C inactivates biology.

View UV Light Sanitizer Installation →

Duct Cleaning - What the NADCA Standard Actually Requires

NADCA defines the professional standard for duct cleaning. AIRONE follows this standard: mechanical agitation to loosen debris from duct walls, combined with HEPA vacuum extraction to capture and remove dislodged particles without releasing them into your home. AIRONE inspects registers, grilles, air handler components, and accessible duct sections before and after service to confirm cleaning effectiveness.

View Duct Cleaning Service →

Duct Sealing - Aeroseal vs. Manual Mastic - Which Method Is Right?

Aeroseal is a pressurized aerosol polymer sealant injected directly into the duct system. It travels to leak points and bonds to seal gaps from the inside - reaching areas physically inaccessible for manual sealing. Aeroseal provides measurable before-and-after leakage data. Manual mastic sealing uses a brush-applied compound to seal visible seams, joints, and connections in accessible duct runs. AIRONE recommends the appropriate method based on your duct system layout, accessibility, and leakage profile.
