Why Maintenance Matters

Why Annual HVAC Maintenance Matters in Southern California

Los Angeles County has one of the longest AC seasons in the United States. From April through October, residential HVAC systems in SoCal run more hours per year than systems in nearly any other US climate region. That extended runtime accelerates component wear - capacitors, contactors, motors, and refrigerant charge levels all degrade faster in high-use systems. According to ACCA, regular HVAC maintenance reduces the likelihood of system breakdown by up to 95%.

What does an annual HVAC tune-up actually include?

A professional AC tune-up includes refrigerant level verification, coil cleaning, capacitor and contactor testing, motor amp draw check, thermostat calibration, condensate drain flush, and a written performance report. A furnace inspection covers heat exchanger inspection, ignition system test, burner cleaning, flue and venting check, and airflow measurement. AIRONE technicians complete both visits with a written report left with the homeowner after every appointment.

How often should an HVAC system be serviced in Los Angeles?

ACCA recommends a minimum of one professional service visit per year for standard residential systems. For homes with year-round heating and cooling demand - which describes most of Los Angeles County, South Bay, and Orange County - two visits per year is the optimal interval. The Silver Plan covers one AC and one furnace visit per year. The Gold Plan adds a second AC visit and a second furnace visit, providing coverage at both ends of the heating and cooling season.

What happens if I skip annual maintenance?

Skipped maintenance allows small performance issues - dirty coils, low refrigerant, worn capacitors - to compound over time. A dirty evaporator coil reduces system efficiency by up to 30 percent and puts added strain on the compressor. A worn capacitor that fails during a summer heatwave results in an emergency no-cool call, which costs significantly more than a preventive tune-up. Most compressor failures are preceded by months of warning signs that a professional tune-up would have caught.

Is a Plan Right for You?

Which Los Angeles Homeowners Benefit Most from a Maintenance Plan?

A maintenance plan delivers the most value for homeowners whose HVAC system is their primary source of cooling or heating - which in Los Angeles means nearly every single-family home and condo in the region.

Homes with older HVAC systems (8 years or more)

Systems over 8 years old have more wear on mechanical components and benefit most from annual professional inspection. The member repair discount on both Silver and Gold plans reduces the cost of parts and labor on any repair identified during or between maintenance visits.

Homeowners who want priority scheduling

Plan members receive priority scheduling over non-member service calls. During peak summer and winter periods, priority scheduling can be the difference between same-week service and a week-long wait.

Homes in coastal South Bay and Orange County

Coastal properties are exposed to higher ambient humidity and salt air - both of which accelerate corrosion on outdoor condenser coils and electrical components. Annual coil cleaning and component inspection is especially important for systems within a few miles of the coast.

Homeowners who want a written record of system performance

Both Silver and Gold plan members receive a written maintenance report after every visit. The report documents system performance, any components flagged for monitoring, and any repairs completed. This record is useful for insurance claims, home sale disclosures, and extended warranty documentation.
