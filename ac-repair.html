AC making loud or unusual noises

Banging, grinding, rattling, or squealing from an AC unit indicates a mechanical failure in progress. A loose blower wheel, failing compressor, or worn fan motor - each gets progressively more expensive to repair the longer the system runs. Call before a $200 repair becomes a $2,000 replacement.

AC not turning on at all

If your AC will not turn on, the issue is likely electrical - a tripped breaker, a blown fuse, a failed capacitor, or a faulty thermostat. Before calling, reset the breaker and check the thermostat batteries. If neither resolves the issue, AIRONE diagnoses the problem same day.

AC short cycling - turning on and off repeatedly

An AC that turns on and off every few minutes is short cycling - a condition that strains the compressor, wastes energy, and signals an underlying problem such as low refrigerant, an oversized system, or a failing capacitor. Left unaddressed, short cycling leads to premature compressor failure.

Weak or no airflow from vents

Weak airflow is typically caused by a clogged air filter, a failing blower motor, or a collapsed duct section. A severely restricted filter can reduce airflow by up to 50% and cause the evaporator coil to freeze - turning a simple filter replacement into a multi-hour thaw and repair job.

Repair vs Replace

Should I Repair or Replace My AC System? How to Decide.

This is the most important question an HVAC technician should help you answer honestly - and the one where conflicts of interest are most common in this industry.

Signs repair is the right choice

  • The system is under 10 years old
  • The repair cost is less than 30–40% of replacement cost
  • The system has no prior history of major component failures
  • The refrigerant type is R-410A - not R-22 which is no longer manufactured
  • Energy bills have not increased significantly over the past 1–2 seasons

Signs replacement may be the better investment

  • The system is over 12–15 years old
  • The repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement cost
  • The system uses R-22 refrigerant - increasingly expensive and difficult to source
  • The compressor has failed - typically not worth replacing on a system over 10 years old
  • Repeated repair history over the past 2–3 years
  • Significantly higher energy bills indicating declining efficiency

The $5,000 Rule

The $5,000 rule is a widely used framework in the HVAC industry - referenced by Consumer Reports and HVAC industry associations as a practical replacement decision guide. Multiply the age of the system in years by the estimated repair cost. If the result exceeds $5,000, replacement typically provides better long-term value. Example: a 12-year-old system facing a $500 repair = $6,000 — replacement is worth serious consideration at that point.

What AIRONE does

After diagnosing your system, your AIRONE technician provides an honest repair vs replace assessment - including the expected remaining lifespan of the system, the cost of the repair vs a comparable new system, and whether any rebates or financing make replacement more accessible. We do not earn more by recommending replacement. Our job is to give you the information you need to make the right decision for your home and budget.
