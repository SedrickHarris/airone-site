Gas Furnace Services

What Does AIRONE's Gas Furnace Service Cover?

AIRONE performs the full range of C-20 licensed gas furnace services - from same-day diagnosis and repair to annual safety inspections and combustion analysis. Here is what each service covers.

🔬

Combustion Analysis - What's actually happening inside your furnace

Combustion analysis measures flue CO concentration, O2 percentage, stack temperature, and combustion efficiency using a calibrated combustion analyzer. A properly tuned gas furnace burning efficiently produces minimal CO in the flue and maximum heat output per cubic foot of gas consumed. AIRONE performs combustion analysis on every gas furnace service visit - not as an upsell, but as the baseline for confirming the system is safe and operating correctly.

🔧

Gas Valve Inspection - The safety-critical component that controls gas flow

The gas valve controls gas flow to the burner assembly and is one of the most safety-critical components in a gas furnace. A failing gas valve may allow gas to flow when the system is off, prevent ignition when heat is called for, or allow incomplete combustion by delivering incorrect gas pressure. AIRONE tests gas valve operation, gas pressure upstream and downstream of the valve, and valve response during ignition sequence on every service call.

Schedule Gas Valve Inspection →
⚠️

Heat Exchanger Inspection - The CO hazard that visual checks alone can miss

A cracked heat exchanger allows combustion gases - including carbon monoxide - to mix with the air circulated through your home. AIRONE uses a combustion analyzer to detect combustion gas spillage from the heat exchanger in addition to visual inspection - a more reliable detection method than visual inspection alone. If a crack is detected, the system is shut down and the homeowner is notified before the technician leaves. Do not continue operating a furnace with a confirmed cracked heat exchanger.

Schedule Heat Exchanger Inspection →
🛡️

Carbon Monoxide Safety Testing - Measured at the flue and at the living space

AIRONE measures CO at the flue connection using the combustion analyzer and at the living space using a calibrated CO detector on every gas furnace service visit. Elevated CO at the living space indicates combustion gas spillage from a cracked heat exchanger, improper venting, or a blocked flue. All CO readings are documented in the written service report. If CO levels are dangerous, the system is shut down immediately.

Schedule CO Safety Check →
🏗️

Flue and Venting - Blocked or deteriorated flues are a silent CO risk

The flue and venting system carries combustion gases from the furnace to the outside of the building. A blocked, disconnected, or deteriorated flue allows combustion gases to back-draft into the living space. AIRONE inspects the full flue path - from the furnace draft hood or inducer outlet through the venting to the exterior termination - on every gas furnace service visit. Common findings: bird nests at flue terminations, corroded B-vent sections, improper vent slope, and disconnected joints.

Schedule Flue Inspection →
✅

Safety Controls - Every safety shutoff tested before the technician leaves

Modern gas furnaces have multiple safety controls: high limit switches, rollout switches, pressure switches, and flame sensors that shut the system down when unsafe conditions are detected. A safety control that has failed in the bypassed position - meaning it no longer shuts the system down when it should - is more dangerous than one that fails off. AIRONE tests every safety control on every service visit and documents the results.

C-20 Licensing

Why C-20 Licensing Matters for Gas Furnace Service

Not every HVAC contractor is licensed for gas appliance work. Here is what C-20 licensing means and why it matters before you allow anyone to service your gas furnace.

What is C-20 classification and what does it cover?

California Contractor State License Board C-20 classification - Warm-Air Heating, Ventilating and Air-Conditioning - covers gas appliance installation, service, and repair in California. A C-20 license means the contractor has passed a California state examination on gas appliance systems, carries the required bond and insurance, and is subject to CSLB oversight. AIRONE holds CA License #114807, C-20 classification. Verify at cslb.ca.gov - search license #114807.

Can an unlicensed contractor legally service my gas furnace in California?

No. Under California Business and Professions Code Section 7028, performing HVAC or gas appliance work for compensation without a valid CSLB license is illegal when the project value exceeds $500. Gas appliance service requires C-20 or C-36 (Plumbing) classification for gas line work. Unlicensed contractors cannot pull permits, cannot be held to CSLB standards, and leave the homeowner with no recourse if the work is performed incorrectly. Always verify contractor license status at cslb.ca.gov before authorizing any gas appliance work.

Does SoCalGas recommend annual gas furnace inspections?

Yes - SoCalGas recommends annual appliance inspections for residential gas furnaces and offers rebates for high-efficiency furnace replacements through their residential rebate programs. Gas furnaces served by SoCalGas in Los Angeles County, South Bay, and most of Orange County benefit from annual combustion analysis and safety checks performed by a C-20 licensed contractor. AIRONE is familiar with SoCalGas service territory requirements and coordinates rebate documentation for qualifying replacement projects.

What is California Mechanical Code and how does it apply to gas furnace service?

California Mechanical Code (CMC) governs the installation, repair, and service of gas appliances in California. CMC Section 1205 requires that gas appliances be installed and maintained to prevent CO hazards. CMC requires proper venting, combustion air supply, and safety control operation for all gas furnaces. AIRONE service visits verify CMC compliance as part of every gas furnace safety assessment - not as a separate service.
