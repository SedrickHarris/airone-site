How It Works

How UV-C Germicidal Light Sanitizers Work Inside an HVAC System

UV-C light inactivates airborne pathogens at the DNA level - a different mechanism from filtration, and one that addresses the biological contaminants that even high-efficiency filters cannot remove.

What does UV-C light actually do to airborne pathogens?

UV-C light operates at a wavelength of approximately 254 nanometers - a frequency that is absorbed by the DNA and RNA of microorganisms including bacteria, viruses, and mold spores. When a microorganism is exposed to sufficient UV-C energy, the absorbed radiation damages its nucleic acids, preventing it from replicating. An organism that cannot replicate cannot cause infection or colonize surfaces. UV-C does not filter or remove organisms from the air - it inactivates them in place as they pass through the UV-C exposure zone inside the air handler.

Where inside the HVAC system does a UV-C sanitizer install?

AIRONE installs UV-C germicidal lamps in two possible positions inside the air handler. Coil-mount installation positions the lamp to shine continuously on the evaporator coil surface - the single most common site of mold and microbial growth in residential HVAC systems, because the coil surface stays wet during cooling operation. Air-stream installation positions the lamp in the supply plenum to irradiate the moving air stream on every pass through the system. Dual-lamp systems combine both positions for the most complete sanitization coverage. AIRONE recommends the appropriate configuration based on your air handler layout and primary IAQ concern.

What is the difference between UV-C sanitizers and air purifiers - which do I need?

UV-C germicidal lights and air purifiers address different categories of indoor air contaminants. Air purifiers - MERV 13 media, HEPA units, and PCO systems - capture or oxidize particles and gases: dust, allergens, smoke particles, VOCs. UV-C sanitizers inactivate biology: bacteria, viruses, and mold spores. The two technologies are complementary, not interchangeable. Filtration captures the particle; UV-C inactivates the organism. For complete IAQ coverage, AIRONE typically recommends MERV 13 or HEPA filtration as the first stage and UV-C sanitization as the second stage - both can be installed in a single visit.

System Configurations

UV-C Sanitizer Configurations - What AIRONE Installs

AIRONE installs UV-C germicidal light systems in two primary configurations. The right configuration depends on your air handler layout, your primary IAQ concern, and whether you want surface sanitization, air stream sanitization, or both.

Single-Lamp Coil-Mount UV-C System

A single UV-C lamp installed to continuously irradiate the evaporator coil surface. The evaporator coil runs wet during AC operation - cool metal surface plus moisture creates ideal conditions for mold, mildew, and microbial biofilm growth. Coil contamination degrades heat transfer efficiency and introduces biological material into the air stream every time the system runs. A coil-mount UV-C lamp continuously sanitizes the coil surface, preventing biological buildup between maintenance visits. This is the most common UV-C configuration AIRONE installs and is the recommended starting point for most homes.

Single-Lamp Air-Stream UV-C System

A single UV-C lamp installed in the supply plenum, positioned to irradiate the air stream moving through the system on every HVAC cycle. Air-stream installation inactivates organisms suspended in the moving air - including those that originated from sources other than the coil, such as return air drawn from occupied spaces. Air-stream UV-C is the appropriate configuration when the primary concern is airborne pathogen load rather than coil surface contamination - for example, in homes with occupants with respiratory conditions or immunocompromised family members.

Dual-Lamp UV-C System - Coil and Air Stream

A dual-lamp system installs one lamp in the coil-mount position and a second lamp in the air-stream position, providing continuous sanitization of both the evaporator coil surface and the moving air stream simultaneously. Dual-lamp systems deliver the most complete UV-C sanitization coverage available in a residential air handler installation. AIRONE recommends the dual-lamp configuration for households with elevated biological IAQ concerns - active mold history, respiratory illness in the household, high occupancy, or homes recovering from water intrusion or flood damage.

SoCal Context

Why UV-C Sanitization Is Relevant for Southern California Homes

Southern California’s climate creates specific conditions that make biological IAQ - mold, mildew, and airborne pathogens - a year-round concern in residential HVAC systems.

Coastal humidity and evaporator coil mold

South Bay and Orange County homes experience coastal humidity year-round. The evaporator coil in a residential air handler runs wet during AC operation - refrigerant temperatures cause moisture in the circulated air to condense on the coil surface. In coastal areas with elevated ambient humidity, this moisture persists longer and coil surfaces stay wet more of the time than in drier inland climates. Without UV-C treatment, this creates favorable conditions for mold and mildew colonization of the coil surface - which then distributes spores through every room in the home on every HVAC cycle.

Post-wildfire biological activity in duct systems

Wildfire smoke carries biological material in addition to fine particles - including fungal spores and bacteria associated with burned organic matter. During smoke events when HVAC systems are running to filter indoor air, this biological material can be deposited inside duct systems and on air handler components. Professional duct cleaning after a significant wildfire event removes the deposited material. UV-C sanitization provides ongoing protection against biological re-establishment in the air handler and at the coil surface during subsequent cooling and heating seasons.

High-occupancy homes and airborne pathogen load

Higher household occupancy increases the airborne pathogen load inside a home - more occupants means more exhaled bacteria and viruses recirculating through the HVAC system. This is relevant for multi-generational households, homes with young children or elderly occupants, and households that have experienced recurring respiratory illness. A UV-C air-stream installation in the supply plenum inactivates recirculating pathogens on every HVAC cycle - reducing the biological load in recirculated air without requiring any change in ventilation habits or occupancy behavior.
