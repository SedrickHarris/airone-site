What's Included

What Does the Gold Maintenance Plan Include?

The Gold Plan is AIRONE's most complete residential HVAC coverage. It provides professional service at both ends of Southern California's heating and cooling seasons - a spring AC visit before peak summer demand, a mid-season or fall AC check, a fall furnace visit before the heating season, and a mid-winter furnace visit. Every visit includes a complete inspection, cleaning, performance verification, and written report. Gold Plan members also receive priority emergency response when a system failure occurs between scheduled visits.

Two AC Visits Per Year - Spring and Mid-Season Coverage

  • First AC visit scheduled March through May - before peak summer demand - catches degraded capacitors, low refrigerant, and dirty coils before the system faces its highest-load months
  • Second AC visit scheduled mid-summer or early fall - verifies performance under load, catches any mid-season charge loss, and prepares the system for the transition out of the cooling season
  • Both visits include the complete AIRONE AC tune-up checklist: electrical system, refrigerant system, mechanical components, coils, airflow, condensate system, and written report
  • Gold members are scheduled before non-member and Silver member calls during peak spring booking periods

Two Furnace Visits Per Year - Fall and Mid-Winter Coverage

  • First furnace visit scheduled September through November - before the heating season - covers heat exchanger inspection, burner cleaning, ignition test, venting check, gas pressure verification, and airflow measurement
  • Second furnace visit scheduled mid-winter - verifies heating performance under load, checks for any mid-season performance degradation, and confirms safety controls are operating correctly
  • Both visits include the complete AIRONE furnace inspection checklist with written maintenance report
  • Particularly valuable for older systems where mid-season heat exchanger fatigue and igniter wear are more likely to occur

Priority Emergency Response - Gold Members First

  • Gold Plan members receive priority emergency dispatch ahead of non-member and Silver Plan service calls when an HVAC system failure occurs
  • Priority emergency response applies 24 hours a day, 7 days a week - including weekends and holidays
  • AIRONE dispatches emergency HVAC service across Los Angeles County, South Bay, and Orange County 24/7
  • Priority emergency response means Gold members are placed at the front of the emergency dispatch queue - minimizing wait time during heatwaves and cold snaps when emergency call volume is highest

15 Percent Member Discount on Parts and Repair Labor

  • 15 percent member discount applies to all parts and repair labor on any service call during the plan year - not limited to scheduled maintenance visits
  • Discount applies whether the repair is identified during a tune-up or results from a separate service call between visits
  • No cap on the number of repair calls eligible for the member discount
  • Gold Plan discount is 5 percentage points higher than the Silver Plan - the difference compounds on any major repair identified during the plan year
  • Discount is applied automatically as a Gold Plan member

Written Maintenance Report After Every Visit

  • Full written condition report provided after every one of the four annual visits - not just the first visit of the year
  • Documents all work performed, all components inspected, all readings taken, and any components flagged for monitoring or repair
  • Four annual reports create a complete documented service history that supports manufacturer warranty compliance, home sale disclosure, insurance documentation, and warranty claim support
  • Most major HVAC brands - Carrier, Trane, Lennox, Rheem, Goodman, and others - require annual professional maintenance to keep equipment warranties valid; Gold Plan reports satisfy that documentation requirement on both the AC and furnace systems

Annual Whole-System Performance Verification

  • Once per year, AIRONE performs a whole-system performance verification that measures the integrated performance of the AC and furnace systems together - not just individual component checks
  • Verification includes supply and return temperature differential across the system, static pressure measurement for duct restriction assessment, airflow balance across all zones, and thermostat response and calibration
  • Results are documented in writing and compared against the prior year's verification to identify any year-over-year performance degradation
  • Identifies duct losses, airflow imbalances, and system efficiency losses that individual component inspections may not surface

Refrigerant Top-Off Up to 1 Lb Per Year - Included

  • Up to 1 lb of refrigerant top-off per year is included in the Gold Plan at no additional parts charge - labor for the recharge procedure is also included
  • Refrigerant charge loss of under 1 lb per year is common in aging systems with minor line set micro-leaks that do not require immediate repair
  • Including the top-off in the plan prevents the gradual charge loss that reduces system efficiency and accelerates compressor wear over time
  • If refrigerant loss exceeds 1 lb or indicates an active leak, the technician identifies the leak source and provides a flat-rate leak repair quote - the member discount applies to any repair work authorized
  • Refrigerant handling is performed by AIRONE's EPA 608 certified technicians
Your Four Annual Visits

What Happens on Each of the Four Gold Plan Visits?

The Gold Plan schedules four professional visits per year - two for the AC system and two for the furnace. Here is what each visit covers and when it is scheduled.

1

Step 1 - Spring AC Visit (March Through May)

The spring AC visit is the most critical tune-up of the year for Southern California homeowners. Scheduled before peak summer demand, this visit catches every component that could fail under the sustained high-load conditions of a Los Angeles summer - capacitors, contactors, refrigerant charge, coil fouling, and motor wear. Any degraded component identified at the spring visit is replaced before the system faces its heaviest months. Gold Plan members are scheduled before non-member and Silver Plan calls during the high-demand spring booking period - securing earlier appointment dates when availability is most limited.

2

Step 2 - Mid-Season or Fall AC Visit (July Through September)

The second AC visit confirms the system is performing correctly under actual summer load conditions and catches any mid-season charge loss, coil fouling from the summer's elevated pollen and particulate levels, or motor wear that has developed since the spring visit. It also prepares the system for the transition out of the cooling season - verifying that the condensate system is clear, the refrigerant charge is within spec, and the system is ready to operate at reduced load through the fall months.

3

Step 3 - Fall Furnace Visit (September Through November)

The fall furnace visit is scheduled before the heating season begins - inspecting and cleaning the heat exchanger, burner assembly, ignition system, flue and venting pathway, gas pressure, blower motor, and all safety controls. A cracked heat exchanger identified at the fall visit is a safety finding that requires attention before the furnace is operated in heating mode. Scheduling the fall furnace visit in September or October gives time to complete any necessary repairs before the first cold nights of the season.

4

Step 4 - Mid-Winter Furnace Visit (December Through February)

The mid-winter furnace visit confirms heating system performance under load - measuring heat rise across the heat exchanger, verifying gas pressure at the manifold under firing conditions, and checking ignition system performance after months of regular heating-season operation. Mid-winter is when ignition component fatigue and heat exchanger stress from repeated thermal cycling are most likely to surface. Gold Plan members receive a written maintenance report after this visit documenting the system's condition at the midpoint of the heating season.

Is Gold Right for You?

Who Is the Gold Maintenance Plan Best For?

The Gold Plan delivers the most value for homeowners whose HVAC system runs heavily across Southern California's long cooling season - or who want the deepest coverage and fastest emergency response available.

Homeowners with older HVAC systems (8 years or more)

Systems over 8 years old have more accumulated wear on capacitors, contactors, motors, heat exchangers, and ignition components. Two visits per system per year - rather than one - doubles the number of opportunities to catch a degrading component before it fails. The 15 percent member discount also provides more savings per repair call as repair frequency increases with system age.

Homeowners in coastal South Bay and Orange County

Coastal properties experience elevated ambient humidity and salt air year-round - both of which accelerate corrosion on outdoor condenser coils, electrical components, and refrigerant line connections. Two AC visits per year means coil cleaning and component inspection happen twice annually for coastal systems, where a single annual visit may not be sufficient to address the elevated rate of corrosion and fouling.

Homeowners who want 24/7 priority emergency response

A summer heatwave in Los Angeles can push indoor temperatures above 100 degrees within hours of an AC failure. Gold Plan members receive priority emergency dispatch ahead of non-member calls - 24 hours a day, 7 days a week including weekends and holidays. For households with elderly residents, young children, or medical needs that make extreme indoor temperatures a health concern, priority emergency response is the most important feature in the plan.

Homeowners who want refrigerant top-off included

Many aging AC systems lose a small amount of refrigerant charge each year through micro-leaks in the line set or coil connections that are below the threshold for immediate repair. A system running slightly low on charge runs less efficiently, works harder, and places additional stress on the compressor. The Gold Plan's annual refrigerant top-off of up to 1 lb keeps charge within manufacturer spec without requiring a separate service call or repair authorization each year.
