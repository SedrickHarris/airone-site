Repair vs Replace

Should I Repair or Replace My Furnace? How to Decide.

This is the most important question an HVAC technician should help you answer honestly - and the one where conflicts of interest are most common in this industry.

Signs repair is the right choice

  • The furnace is under 12 years old
  • The repair cost is less than 30–40% of replacement cost
  • The furnace has no prior history of major component failures
  • The heat exchanger is intact - no cracks or CO concerns identified
  • Energy bills have not increased significantly over the past 1–2 heating seasons

Signs replacement may be the better investment

  • The furnace is over 15 years old
  • The repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement cost
  • The heat exchanger has a crack - a CO hazard and typically not worth repairing
  • The furnace uses an R-22 or older refrigerant system paired with it (relevant for combined AC + furnace decisions)
  • Repeated repair history over the past 2–3 years
  • Significantly higher gas bills indicating declining AFUE efficiency

The $5,000 Rule

The $5,000 rule is a widely used framework in the HVAC industry - referenced by Consumer Reports and HVAC industry associations as a practical replacement decision guide. Multiply the age of the furnace in years by the estimated repair cost. If the result exceeds $5,000, replacement typically provides better long-term value. Example: a 14-year-old furnace facing a $450 repair = $6,300 - replacement is worth serious consideration at that point.

Replacement Options

What Should I Replace My Furnace With? Options, Costs, and Honest Advice.

AIRONE presents options at every efficiency tier - not a single upsell. Here are the four replacement paths most relevant to Los Angeles homeowners, with honest guidance on when each makes sense.

Single-Stage Gas Furnace (80% AFUE) - Like-for-like replacement: lowest upfront cost, adequate for mild SoCal winters.

A single-stage 80% AFUE furnace is the most direct replacement for an aging gas furnace. It has the lowest upfront cost and is adequate for Southern California's mild winters where annual heating hours are low compared to colder US climates. The trade-off is the highest long-term gas cost of any replacement option - 20 cents of every gas dollar is lost through exhaust. Best for: budget-constrained replacements, rental properties, and homeowners planning to sell within 5 years.

See Furnace Installation →

Two-Stage Gas Furnace (80–96% AFUE) - The most popular replacement choice for SoCal homeowners staying 5+ years.

A two-stage furnace runs at 60–65% capacity most of the time and kicks to 100% only on cold nights - producing 10–15% lower gas consumption than a single-stage unit. The efficiency upgrade premium is typically $500–$1,500 over minimum efficiency and pays back in 4–6 years through lower SoCalGas bills. At replacement time - when labor and permit costs are already sunk - the incremental cost of a higher efficiency tier is at its lowest relative to long-term return. AIRONE provides a written efficiency payback calculation for every replacement quote at each efficiency tier.

Variable-Speed Gas Furnace (95–98% AFUE) - Maximum efficiency. IRS §25C federal tax credit up to $600 per year.

A variable-speed 95–98% AFUE furnace modulates continuously - matching real-time heat loss rather than cycling on/off. The result is the quietest, most efficient, and most precise heating available in a gas system. Qualifies for the IRS Form 5695 §25C federal tax credit - up to $600 per year under the Inflation Reduction Act (2023–2032). Also qualifies for SoCalGas high-efficiency rebates - check current amounts at socalgas.com/rebates. Best for: homeowners planning to stay 7+ years who want the maximum rebate stack and lowest long-term gas cost.

Heat Pump - Replace Furnace and AC: Replace both systems in one installation. $2,000 IRA federal tax credit eligible.

If the AC system is also aging - within 3–5 years of the furnace - a heat pump replaces both the furnace and the AC in a single installation, sharing the permit and labor cost rather than paying twice. Heat pumps operate at 250–350% efficiency in SoCal's mild climate - far exceeding any gas furnace option. Qualifies for the $2,000 IRS Form 5695 §25C federal tax credit and TECH Clean California rebates up to $3,000 through SoCalGas and SCE. AIRONE provides combined AC + furnace replacement quotes and a separate heat pump quote when this decision is relevant to your situation.
