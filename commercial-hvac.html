Section 4 - Rooftop Units

Commercial Rooftop AC Unit (RTU) Service in Los Angeles

Rooftop AC units (RTUs) are the most common commercial HVAC configuration in Los Angeles - used in restaurants, retail spaces, medical offices, flat-roof homes, and most single-story commercial buildings. AIRONE's commercial RTU service covers preventive maintenance, repair, and full replacement.

What does AIRONE's commercial RTU service include?

RTU service includes scheduled preventive maintenance (filter replacement, coil cleaning, refrigerant charge verification, blower and motor inspection, control system check, economizer service), refrigerant service per EPA Section 608 compliance, blower and motor repair or replacement, control system diagnosis, condenser coil cleaning and replacement, and full RTU replacement when end-of-life is reached. AIRONE technicians are equipped with appropriate rooftop safety equipment and OSHA fall protection certification.

When does a commercial RTU need to be replaced?

Commercial RTUs typically last 12 to 17 years with maintenance and 8 to 10 years without. Replace when: the unit is approaching or past 12 years and faces a major component failure (compressor, heat exchanger, condenser coil), repair costs exceed 50% of a comparable replacement, the system uses R-22 refrigerant (production stopped in 2020), or recurring repairs over the past 2 to 3 years exceed $1,500. AIRONE provides a written repair vs replace assessment after every diagnostic on aging commercial RTUs.

What commercial RTU brands does AIRONE service and install?

AIRONE services and installs all major commercial RTU brands including Carrier, Trane, Lennox, Rheem, York, Goodman, Daikin, and other brands installed on commercial properties throughout LA County, South Bay, and Orange County. Section 5 - VRF & Advanced Commercial Systems

VRF Systems, Commercial Mini Splits, and Commercial Heat Pumps

What is a VRF system and when is it the right commercial HVAC choice?

VRF (Variable Refrigerant Flow) is a commercial HVAC configuration where one outdoor condenser supplies refrigerant to multiple indoor units - each providing independent zone control without ductwork. According to ASHRAE Standard 90.1, VRF systems can deliver 20 to 30% energy savings compared to conventional split or package systems in mild climates like Southern California. VRF is particularly well-suited to multi-tenant office buildings, mixed-use developments, hotels, medical office suites, and any commercial property where diverse zone loads or independent zone control are required without the ductwork complexity of a central system.

Section 11 - Permits & Title 24

Commercial HVAC Permits and Title 24 Compliance in California

Commercial HVAC installation and replacement in California requires building permits and Title 24 commercial energy code compliance documentation. Title 24 commercial provisions are more stringent than residential and include specific requirements for economizers, demand-controlled ventilation, and duct leakage testing.

Does commercial HVAC installation require a permit in California?

Yes - commercial HVAC installation, replacement, and major repair require building permits across every city in Los Angeles County, South Bay, and Orange County. The permit triggers a building inspection at completion verifying compliance with California Building Code, electrical code, and Title 24 commercial energy efficiency standards. AIRONE files all required permits, pays the permit fees, and coordinates the post-install inspection. You do not manage any part of the commercial permit process.

What does Title 24 require for commercial HVAC installations?

California's Title 24 commercial provisions are more detailed than residential and include four key requirements: (1) economizer requirements - commercial systems above specific capacity thresholds must include a properly sized and functional outside air economizer. (2) Demand-controlled ventilation (DCV) - for spaces with variable occupancy (conference rooms, restaurants, retail), systems must include CO2-based or occupancy-based ventilation control. (3) Duct leakage testing - commercial duct systems must test below specific leakage thresholds verified during the building inspection. (4) Refrigerant charge verification - documentation that the installation was commissioned to manufacturer specifications. AIRONE's commercial installation methodology is designed to pass Title 24 inspection on the first visit.

How does AIRONE handle the commercial permit process?

AIRONE files commercial building permits with the city or county within 1 to 2 business days of contract approval, pays all permit fees as line items in the proposal, and manages the entire process through inspection. Commercial permit processing varies by city - most LA County and OC commercial permits process within 5 to 15 business days, longer for complex VRF or new-construction projects. AIRONE provides Title 24 compliance documentation including economizer verification, DCV setup confirmation, duct leakage test results, refrigerant charge verification, and licensed contractor sign-off - all included in the post-install documentation package.
