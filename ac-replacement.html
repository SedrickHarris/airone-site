Is a 12–15 year old AC system worth replacing?

Yes - a central AC system in Southern California typically lasts 12–17 years with annual maintenance and 8–10 years without, according to industry service life data. Should I replace an AC system that uses R-22 refrigerant?

Section 3 - Repair vs Replace

Is It Better to Repair or Replace Your AC System in Los Angeles?

The short answer: Replace your AC when two or more of these four conditions apply - the system is over 12 years old, the repair cost exceeds 50% of a comparable new system, the system uses R-22 refrigerant, or recurring repairs over 2–3 years have reached $1,000–$1,500. If only one condition applies, repair is usually the right call.

This is the most important financial decision on this page - and the one where most homeowners want a straight answer rather than a sales pitch. AIRONE's answer depends on four variables specific to your system and your situation.

Variable 1 - How old is your system?

Age is the most reliable single predictor of replacement value. Systems under 8 years old with a single component failure almost always make more financial sense to repair. Systems over 12 years old with any major component failure - compressor, condenser coil, evaporator coil - almost always make sense to replace. The 8–12 year range is where the other three variables determine the right answer.

Variable 2 - What does the repair cost as a percentage of replacement?

AIRONE uses the 50% rule - referenced by Consumer Reports - as the primary financial threshold: if the repair costs more than 50% of a comparable new replacement system, replacement delivers better long-term value in virtually every scenario. A $2,500 repair on a system where replacement costs $5,000 is the financial equivalent of buying the system twice - once at full price and once through repair costs - without the efficiency gains, warranty protection, or reliability of new equipment.

Variable 3 - What refrigerant does your system use?

R-22 systems face a different calculus than R-410A systems. Because R-22 cannot be converted and its supply is permanently finite, an R-22 system that requires refrigerant service should be evaluated for replacement regardless of age - particularly in Southern California where the long cooling season makes refrigerant-related wear more likely than in cooler climates.

Variable 4 - What is your system's repair history?

A system's repair history over the past 2–3 years is the second most reliable predictor of future failure frequency. A system that has required $1,500 in repairs over 3 years - even if each individual repair was technically justified - is demonstrating a failure cascade pattern that replacement resets entirely.

The honest answer:

If your system scores replacement on two or more of these four variables, AIRONE will tell you that replacement makes more financial sense - and provide a written quote before you commit to anything. If your system scores repair on three or more variables, AIRONE will tell you that too. AIRONE technicians do not earn more by recommending replacement over repair. The recommendation is based on the math, not the margin.

Section 8 - R-22 Replacement

Replacing an R-22 System in Los Angeles - What You Need to Know

R-22 refrigerant replacement affects a significant portion of Los Angeles County homes - particularly those with systems installed between 2000 and 2010. If your system uses R-22, replacement planning should begin now - not after the next refrigerant leak.

Why does an R-22 AC system require replacement planning?

R-22 systems require immediate replacement planning because R-22 production was permanently stopped in 2020 - remaining supplies are being depleted and refrigerant service costs are rising sharply as availability decreases. The EPA phased out R-22 production and import under Section 605(a) of the Clean Air Act. Unlike earlier phase-down periods, this was a complete production stop - R-22 is no longer manufactured in the United States. A Southern California AC system that uses R-22 and develops a refrigerant leak in 2026 faces a dramatically different repair economics equation than the same leak in 2015 - and that equation will continue to worsen every year as remaining supplies decline.

Can an R-22 system be converted to R-410A or R-454B?

No. R-22 systems cannot be converted to R-410A or R-454B. These refrigerants operate at significantly higher pressures than R-22 - pressures that R-22 system components were not designed or certified to handle. Attempting to run R-410A in an R-22 system risks compressor failure, coil rupture, and refrigerant release. The only correct solution for an R-22 system that needs refrigerant service is full system replacement with equipment designed for current refrigerants.

How do I know if my AC system uses R-22?

The refrigerant type is printed on the system's nameplate - located on the outdoor condenser unit. R-22 systems show "R-22" or "HCFC-22" on the nameplate. Systems manufactured after approximately 2010 are more likely to use R-410A. If the system was installed before 2010 and you are unsure of the refrigerant type, call (323) 471-1037 - AIRONE identifies the refrigerant type during a free assessment visit.

What does R-22 AC replacement cost in Los Angeles?

The cost to replace an R-22 system with a modern R-410A or R-454B system follows the same replacement cost ranges as any standard replacement - $4,000–$7,000 for a standard central split system depending on size, brand, and efficiency tier. There is no R-22 surcharge on the replacement itself. The financial urgency of R-22 replacement comes from avoiding escalating refrigerant service costs - not from any price premium on the replacement system.
