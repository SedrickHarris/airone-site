Symptom Triggers

Is Your Mini-Split Doing Any of This?

Every symptom below is diagnosable and repairable by a licensed technician on the first visit in most cases. AIRONE charges an $89 diagnostic fee — applied toward the repair cost if you proceed.

❄️

Mini-Split Not Cooling

Indoor head running but no cool air. Most common causes: low refrigerant from a line set or coil connection leak, a failed capacitor in the outdoor condenser, or a dirty evaporator coil on the indoor head restricting airflow across the coil. The system may still run and circulate air — but without cooling output.

Get Same-Day Diagnosis →
💧

Mini-Split Leaking Water

Water dripping or streaming from the indoor head unit is almost always a blocked condensate drain line. Condensate builds up during cooling and drains through a small tube — when the tube clogs with algae or dust, water backs up and overflows. A frozen evaporator coil from restricted airflow or low refrigerant can also produce water leaks when it thaws.

Get Same-Day Diagnosis →
⚠️

Error Code on Display

Most mini-split brands display a fault code on the indoor unit LED panel or remote when a problem is detected. Common codes indicate refrigerant pressure faults, communication failures between indoor and outdoor units, sensor failures, or blocked condensate drainage. The error code significantly narrows the diagnosis. AIRONE technicians are trained on Mitsubishi, Daikin, LG, Fujitsu, and all major brand code systems.

Get Same-Day Diagnosis →
🔊

Mini-Split Making Noise

Mini-splits are engineered for near-silent operation. Rattling typically indicates a cracked or warped indoor fan blade, a loose panel vibrating against the unit housing, or debris inside the indoor air handler. Grinding or squealing from the outdoor unit typically indicates a failing condenser fan motor bearing. Any audible noise beyond low airflow hum warrants a diagnostic visit.

Get Same-Day Diagnosis →
🌡️

Mini-Split Not Heating

On heat pump variant mini-splits, failure to heat is often caused by a defrost cycle fault, a failed reversing valve, or low refrigerant preventing the refrigeration cycle from reversing into heating mode. In SoCal's mild winters, mini-split heat pumps operate at high efficiency — a heating failure is always worth repairing rather than tolerating.

Get Same-Day Diagnosis →
📡

Remote or Controls Not Responding

A mini-split that does not respond to remote commands may have a failed indoor unit circuit board, a dead remote IR receiver, or a communication wiring fault between the indoor and outdoor units. Modern mini-splits with WiFi control may also lose connectivity after a router change or firmware update. AIRONE diagnoses both hardware and communication faults on the same visit.

Repair vs Replace

Should I Repair or Replace My Mini-Split?

AIRONE gives you both cost figures at every diagnostic visit. Here is the framework for making the right decision.

Repair Makes Sense When…

  • System is under 10 years old and in otherwise good condition
  • Repair cost is under 40% of the cost of a new replacement system
  • No history of repeated refrigerant leaks or board failures
  • The failed component is a standard part available same-day
  • System uses current refrigerant (R-410A or R-32) — not a discontinued type
  • Single failure with no other underlying system degradation found at diagnostic

Replacement Makes Sense When…

  • System is over 12 years old and facing compressor failure
  • Repair cost approaches or exceeds 50% of a new AHRI-matched system
  • System has had 2 or more repairs totaling $1,000+ in the past 3 years
  • Coil has a leak that requires coil replacement — at that cost, a new system is often comparable
  • System uses an older refrigerant that is difficult or expensive to source
  • Upgrading to a heat pump variant qualifies for IRA §25C tax credit up to $2,000
AIRONE provides both repair cost and full replacement cost at every diagnostic visit — no pressure in either direction. Our job is to give you the information to make the right decision for your home and budget. Call (323) 471-1037 to schedule a diagnostic.
