Symptom Triggers

Is Your Furnace Doing Any of This?

❄️

Furnace running but not heating

Igniter failure is the single most common cause of furnaces running but producing no heat in Southern California. The blower may still circulate air through the vents, but the burners never light. Diagnosable and repairable same-day in most cases - igniter replacement typically takes under 2 hours.

Get Same-Day Furnace Repair →
🔄

Furnace short-cycling - turning on and off repeatedly

A furnace that turns on and off every few minutes is short-cycling. The most common causes are a dirty flame sensor, a clogged air filter causing overheating, or a failing heat exchanger. Short-cycling stresses every component in the system. Cleaning the flame sensor often resolves it for $100–$200.

Get Same-Day Furnace Repair →
💥

Banging or booming noise on startup

A loud bang or boom when the furnace fires up is typically caused by delayed ignition - gas accumulates in the combustion chamber and ignites all at once when the igniter finally fires. This is hard on the heat exchanger and can crack it over time. Have it diagnosed before it becomes a CO hazard.

Get Same-Day Furnace Repair →
🔊

Clicking noise that won't stop

Continuous clicking with no ignition usually indicates an igniter trying and failing to spark, a faulty control board signaling for ignition repeatedly, or a flame sensor failing to confirm flame. Each diagnosis is different - the fix is typically under $300 if caught early.

Get Same-Day Furnace Repair →
🔥

Yellow or orange pilot light - should be blue

A healthy furnace flame is steady blue. Yellow or orange flames indicate incomplete combustion - which produces carbon monoxide. Yellow flame is an urgent safety issue. Turn the furnace off, ensure your CO detector is functioning, and call AIRONE same-day for inspection and repair.

Get Same-Day Furnace Repair →
🌧️

Blowing cold air instead of warm

Cold air from the vents while the furnace is running typically indicates the burners are not lighting, the flame sensor is dirty, or a safety limit switch has tripped due to overheating. The blower runs regardless of whether the burners are lit. Diagnosable in under an hour in most cases.

Get Same-Day Furnace Repair →
⚡

Furnace won't turn on - no ignition

If the furnace shows no signs of life - no fan, no ignition, no thermostat response - check the circuit breaker, the furnace power switch (often on the unit itself), and the thermostat batteries before calling. If those are all functional, the issue is typically a failed control board, a tripped pressure switch, or a thermostat wiring problem.

Get Same-Day Furnace Repair →
⚠️

Burning smell or carbon monoxide detector triggered

A burning smell from the furnace at season start can be normal dust burn-off during the first few minutes. If it persists, turn the furnace off - it may indicate electrical damage or oil-soaked components. If your CO detector triggers, leave the building immediately and call 911. Then call AIRONE at (323) 471-1037 for inspection.
