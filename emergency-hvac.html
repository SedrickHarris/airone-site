Safety Guidance

Before the Technician Arrives - Emergency HVAC Safety Steps

These steps may resolve minor issues before your technician arrives - potentially saving you the diagnostic fee. If they do not resolve the problem, they help your technician diagnose faster when they arrive.

If your AC stops working during a heat event

Turn the system off at the thermostat to prevent further damage to the compressor from short-cycling. Close blinds and curtains on sun-facing windows to slow heat gain. Move to the lowest floor of the home - heat rises. If anyone in the home is elderly, very young, or has a medical condition, prioritize getting them to a cooled space while you wait for the technician. Then call AIRONE at (323) 471-1037 - most LA County emergency AC calls are dispatched within 2–4 hours.

If this doesn't resolve the issue, call (323) 471-1037 →

If your furnace stops working during cold weather

Turn the thermostat to off rather than leaving it calling for heat - this prevents the system from attempting repeated failed ignition cycles that can damage the control board. Check the circuit breaker panel for a tripped breaker on the furnace circuit - approximately 15% of "furnace not working" calls are resolved by resetting a tripped breaker. If the breaker is fine and the furnace still will not start, call AIRONE.

If this doesn't resolve the issue, call (323) 471-1037 →

If you smell burning from your HVAC system

Turn the system off immediately at the thermostat and at the disconnect switch or breaker. Do not run a system with a burning smell - electrical faults and failed motors can progress to component damage. A burning smell that persists beyond 60 seconds of operation should always be treated as an emergency. Call AIRONE at (323) 471-1037 immediately.

Call (323) 471-1037 now →

If your AC is leaking water

Turn the system off to prevent continued water discharge. Place towels or a container under the unit to protect flooring and ceilings below. Check the condensate drain pan - if it is full, the drain line is blocked. Do not attempt to clear the drain line with a vacuum or compressed air without knowing where the line terminates. Call AIRONE for safe same-day clearing.

If this doesn't resolve the issue, call (323) 471-1037 →

If your thermostat is blank or unresponsive

Check the thermostat batteries first - many thermostats run on AA or AAA batteries that are separate from the HVAC system's power. Dead batteries are the single most common cause of a blank thermostat screen. If new batteries do not restore the display, check the circuit breaker for the air handler or furnace. If both checks fail to restore the thermostat, the issue may be a control board or low-voltage wiring fault. Call AIRONE - diagnosable same day.

If this doesn't resolve the issue, call (323) 471-1037 →
